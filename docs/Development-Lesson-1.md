**PIP** = _*Percantage in Points* or *Price intretst Points*_
- PIP's are measures of unit in a market

| Lot Size | PIP | Dollar Amount | Formual
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | 
| Standard Lot | 1 | $10 | 30 - 20 * 100
| Mini Lot | 1 | $1 | 30 - 20 * 0.1
| Micro Lot | 1 | $0.10 | 30 - 20 * 0.01

Contracts is just a **multiplier**. It multiplies the pip size.

- So if you get 4 contracts in a standard lot and you go long at 4013 and your T/P is at 4000 that would be 13 pip in a standard lot, but the multiplier brings 52. 
    - _Formula is 13 * 2_

_For majority of all currency (USD/EUR) **exluding JPY**, 1 pip is the 4th decimal number count_
- Example: 
    - USD/JPY enter a long at 151.31 stop loss at 151.52  
        - 52 - 32 = 20 so it is 20 pips

### Understanding the interest rate market
- The intrest rate controls the market
- Each country has its own interest rate, which control the market

It's **VERY IMPORTANT** to track countries interest rates
- Overnight Lending Rates
    - Sometimes called the policy rate, federal funds rate in the U.S., or Bank Rate in the U.K.
    - When rates are low, money is cheap â†’ more liquidity â†’ markets often trend up (risk-on).
    - When rates are high, money is expensive â†’ less liquidity â†’ slower markets or sharper corrections (risk-off).
        - Fed raises rates â†’ institutional traders rotate money into bonds or cash â†’ equities drop â†’ volatility spikes.
        - ðŸ’¥ This is why <u>**FOMC announcement**</u> days (and speeches by central bankers) are among the most volatile days for day trading.
    - The **Overnight Lending Rate** is how much a bank charges another bank on a loan to close their day
        - Each commercial bank must hold a minimum amount of capital within their vault or at the FRB in order to avoid penalities.
            - a percentage of its deposits, called the reserve requirement currently 0% in the U.S. since 2020 â€” but banks still manage internal liquidity
        - When a bank is short for the day, the bank contacts another bank for a loan, and then the loan is charged at an interest rate, which is determined by the FED. The rate is usually a range, and the cost is between the rate
            - As of 10/31/2025 3.75% and 4%
                - So banks can loan other banks money with intrest rate between 3.75% - 4%
        - If the rate is too high for the bank, then a percentile of the rate is passed on to consumers and businesses via mortgages, credit cards, loans and etc
